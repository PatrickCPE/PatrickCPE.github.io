<h3> Clubs </h3>
WARNING: Brace for a rant

One thing I was really excited for was to start an embedded club at the school. I reached out during the start of Covid to see if they still offer funding for clubs (pretty much wanted to ship everyone dev boards and hop on calls to learn) and they told me no.

When Covid ended(ended seems far from the right word) in the spring, and we were promised to have in person classes I reached out to the school in early June with all my paperwork and application to start the club and that should of been it.

The school is blatantly incompetent when it comes to handling clubs. Here's the highlights for how it went.

* No reply to multiple emails sent (2 months for any reply)
* They missed the meetings that they set up
* They say everything looked good, and they'd be getting it set up, and then ghost me despite reaching out multiple time
* After 3 months of zero response they respond, ask me to join a meeting with an hours notice, and take a final vote on the club with the student government
* They approve (Hooray!) ... except they never followed up after saying they approved. I asked about it, and was again ghosted
* I officially decide to stop dealing with the school and just buy the stuff for my friends and I in the club out of pocket (say bye to all the fancy Zynq boards I wanted to buy on UML's dime)

Stuff like this makes you appreciate what a good manager does for you behind closed doors. I just wanted to teach some folks and play with some cool tech :(


On other hands, the robotics club finally got some legitimate funding this year so I can splurge on the stuff I design for them :)
